Below is a checklist of essential Do's and Don'ts for maintaining a responsible, safe, and professional social media presence:
The "Do's" (Best Practices)
    1) Do curate a professional digital footprint. Share your coding projects, hackathon prototypes, or club activities to actively build a positive online portfolio.
    2) Do audit your privacy settings regularly. Ensure you understand who can see your posts, photos, and personal details on every platform you use.
    3) Do engage constructively with communities. Use platforms to network with tech groups, campus volunteer organizations, and academic peers.
    4) Do practice the "Front Page Rule." Before posting, ask yourself: Would I be comfortable if this was on the front page of a newspaper or seen by a hiring manager?
    5) Do set healthy boundaries for screen time. Utilize app timers to ensure scrolling does not interfere with your study schedule, project deadlines, or sleep.

The "Don'ts" (Risks to Avoid)
    1) Don't overshare real-time location data. Avoid posting your exact whereabouts or daily routines, as this poses a physical security risk.
    2) Don't post unprofessional or compromising content. Venting about professors, sharing inappropriate photos, or using offensive language can instantly derail internship opportunities.
    3) Don't engage in toxic online arguments. Avoid getting dragged into inflammatory comment sections or cyberbullying; always maintain your professionalism.
    4) Don't rely solely on feeds for accurate information. Social media algorithms create echo chambers. Always fact-check news before sharing it to avoid spreading misinformation.
    5) Don't click on unverified links. Even on social media, be wary of phishing attempts or suspicious direct messages containing links from unknown users.
