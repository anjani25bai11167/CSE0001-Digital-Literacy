Case Study:  Phishing 

**What is Phishing?**
Phishing is a cyberattack where malicious actors impersonate trusted entities to deceive individuals into revealing sensitive information, such as login credentials, personal identification, or financial data.

**The Scenario (Step-by-Step):**
In this realistic fictional scenario, an attacker targets undergraduate students during the chaotic start of a new semester.

**The Hook:** The attacker sends an email spoofing the university's official IT administration. The subject line creates immediate panic: "URGENT: Mandatory Student Portal Verification Required."

**The Bait:** The message claims that the student's access to academic records, lecture materials, and campus Wi-Fi will be permanently suspended within 24 hours unless they log in to update their profile.

**The Trap:** The student clicks the embedded link, which directs them to a fraudulent webpage designed to perfectly mimic the university’s actual login infrastructure.

**The Capture:** Rushed and anxious about losing system access, the student enters their official ID and password. The fake site logs these credentials into the attacker's database and seamlessly redirects the student to the legitimate university homepage, leaving them completely unaware of the theft.

**Typical Targets**
While corporate employees are frequent targets, attackers heavily target university ecosystems. Undergraduates are particularly vulnerable as they are frequently overwhelmed with administrative emails and may not be fully accustomed to verifying official institutional communication channels.

**The Consequences**
Once the attacker possesses valid credentials, they gain unauthorized entry into the internal network. This compromise can lead to the alteration of academic records, the exposure of sensitive peer data, or targeted financial fraud if the compromised account is linked to tuition portals or campus-based e-commerce platforms.
