Maintaining a secure digital presence is essential, especially when navigating a high-tech environment like VIT Bhopal. Whether you are managing academic projects like HostelMart or using common campus services, your digital safety relies on consistent, proactive habits.

    1)UPI PIN Principle: Always remember that your UPI PIN is only required for sending money or checking your balance. If someone asks you to enter your PIN to "receive" a prize or "verify" a refund, it is a scam.

    2)QR Code Caution: Never scan a QR code to receive money. Scammers often send QR codes claiming they are "payment vouchers." Scanning these and entering your PIN will immediately debit your account instead of crediting it.

    3)Campus Wi-Fi & VPNs: While using public or campus Wi-Fi in hostels or labs, avoid accessing sensitive accounts like net banking. If necessary, use a reputable Virtual Private Network (VPN) to encrypt your traffic and protect your data from being intercepted.

    4)Institutional Phishing Awareness: Be wary of emails that appear to be from university administration or "Hostel Management" requesting urgent password resets or fee payments via unverified links. Always check the sender's actual email domain (e.g., ensuring it ends strictly in @vitbhopal.ac.in.

    5)Granular App Permissions: When installing local delivery, hostel-related, or utility apps, review their permission requests. A simple calculator or utility app does not need access to your contacts, SMS, or gallery.

    6)Secure Developer Workflows: For platforms like GitHub or Kaggle, enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) using an authenticator app rather than just SMS. This adds a critical layer of security for your professional portfolio and project code.

    7)Browser Safety Cues: Before entering any credentials on a website—especially university portals—ensure the URL begins with https:// and shows a padlock icon. This indicates the connection is encrypted.

    8)Social Media Privacy: Regularly audit the privacy settings on your social media accounts. Limit the visibility of your personal details, such as your phone number or current location, to "Friends Only" to prevent bad actors from gathering info for social engineering.

**Reporting Cyber Crimes:**
If you encounter a digital scam or financial fraud, immediate reporting is critical.
National Helpline Number: 1930 (Call immediately for financial frauds to increase the chances of freezing the stolen funds).
